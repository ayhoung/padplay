# Voice Rules — Not Sounding Like an LLM

These apply to all marketing copy, Reddit posts, and any public-facing text.

## Banned Patterns

### Rhetorical triple structures
- "No X, no Y, no Z" — dead giveaway
- "X, Y, and Z" as a sentence unto itself to sound punchy
- Three parallel adjectives or clauses stacked for rhythm

### Curated-list intros
- "Here are some that..."
- "A few that stood out..."
- "These are games where..."
- "Here's what held up..."
- Any sentence that frames the following list as a deliberate presentation

### Fake-casual intensifiers
- "genuinely" (the single biggest LLM tell — kill it on sight)
- "shockingly"
- "surprisingly" (when used to feign organic discovery)
- "arguably" (hedge word that sounds like a thesaurus)

### Contrast crutches
- "Not just X — it's Y"
- "Not a stripped down version"
- "It's not X, it's the actual Y"
- Anything that sets up a negative then flips to a positive in the same sentence

### Overly clean structure
- Every game blurb being the exact same length
- Every blurb following **Name** — one sentence description. Two sentence opinion. pattern
- Uniform paragraph sizes throughout
- Em-dash as the only punctuation between a game title and its description

### Marketing vocabulary
- "masterpiece"
- "premium"
- "curated"
- "flagship"
- "polished to a shine"
- "the gold standard"
- "benchmark"
- "stands out"
- "game-changer"

### The "forgetting you're on X" trope
- "you forget you're on a tablet"
- "you forget it's a mobile game"
- Any variation of this

### Fake personal anecdotes that read as constructed
- "My partner who doesn't play games spent two hours on this" — fine once, suspect if it becomes a pattern
- Anecdotes should be specific and messy, not neat little proof points

## What Sounds Real

- Sentences that start mid-thought
- Opinions that aren't perfectly balanced ("this is great BUT" — real people sometimes just say it's great or just say it sucks)
- Mentioning a specific complaint or limitation without immediately softening it
- Uneven paragraph lengths — some games get three sentences, some get one
- Casual punctuation. Periods instead of em-dashes. Starting sentences with "And" or "Also"
- Referencing how you actually found the game or when you played it
- Being wrong or uncertain about something ("I think this is on Android too but I'm not sure")
- Typos and rough edges (add these manually before posting, don't write them in drafts)
- Trailing off or ending a blurb without a neat bow
